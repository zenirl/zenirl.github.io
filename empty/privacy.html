Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 16, 2026

Introduction

Empty ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our mobile application Empty ("the App").

Information We Collect

We do not collect any personal information.

Empty is designed with privacy as a core principle. All your journal entries are stored locally on your device and are never transmitted to our servers or any third parties.

Data Stored Locally on Your Device

  • Journal Entries: Your daily one-sentence entries are stored exclusively on your device using Android's DataStore.
  • App Preferences: Settings such as onboarding completion status are stored locally.
  • No Account Required: Empty does not require you to create an account or provide any personal information.

Information We Do NOT Collect

  • Personal identification information (name, email, phone number)
  • Location data
  • Device identifiers
  • Usage analytics or tracking data
  • Any content you write in the app

Data Sharing

We do not share any data with third parties because we do not collect any data.

Data Storage and Security

  • All data is stored locally on your device
  • Data is not backed up to cloud services by the app itself
  • If you uninstall the app, all locally stored data will be deleted
  • We recommend using your device's built-in backup features if you wish to preserve your entries

Children's Privacy

Empty does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. The app is suitable for users of all ages.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.

Your Rights

Since we do not collect any personal data, there is no personal data to access, modify, or delete from our systems. All your data remains on your device under your control.
